Jekyll Themes?

08 Apr 2013

Update: 25 Sep 2012

I decided it was too much work to add a new theme, and rather not destroy the joy of redesigning blogs by constraining myself with frameworks, so.. from now on the themes will be in the repository of this blog, meaning if you do want to use it, it’s going to take you some setup time, migrating posts or however you do it.

One thing remains to be true: you’re all welcome to use the themes I made, edit it however you like, only that I will not be doing support.

Browse the design changes using this neat release view of the repository. ♥

Update: 20 May 2013

As Jekyll 1.0 released this post is getting a lot more traffic, therefore just so you know upfront, I made a stand-alone theme Scribble which is available on GitHub. And, I am now working on Wardrobe, which would be a jekyll setup with 3-5 themes to start with, it is currently work in progress but you can have a sneak preview here, of one of the themes.

Follow me on Twitter or GitHub to keep yourself in the loop. ♥

This is Mu-An. Over.

It’s a common habit for designers to redesign their sites once every when-I-feel-like-it. It could have been a month since the last redesign, but it might well have been only a day. I suffer from this horrible habit of mine aggressively.

Ever since I started using Jekyll (that’s about 50 days ago), there had been four themes. Yes, four. And during this period I have also made two themes for Rob’s blog and another one for I found this helped. I spend way too much time on these things, but at the same time I do really love doing it, and Jekyll is extremely easy to work with!

Now, I am thinking instead of leaving the old themes buried in my commit logs, I should collect them all and make them available for all you current and future jekyll users with a gem/in a repository.

I think this could be pretty sweet. I want to make more themes and let you use which one ever you feel like on the day easily. Let me know whether and how much better this would make your life in the comments or just tweet at me, and when I get cracking I can then ask a little bird to tell you.

At the moment I still have very little idea yet as how it should work, perhaps I will make a gem for it, or perhaps not, let me know if you have any thoughts on the practicalities.

It’s time for us all to blog like a hacker with an endless wardrobe.

Til next time,
Mu-An at 15:53
